	## Backstory
	The house Tarrant is a noble family. Salynna is very much not a noble character. At 15 she had already made herself an absolute headache at home, failing to learn her noble etiquette, kissing boys from the wrong social classes behind the stables and having a bad habit of stealing silverware. She was also growing unreasonably strong for her age, and decidedly unladylike to boot: tall, muscular and curvy, a dominant physique more suited for a rowdy tavern than the royal court.
	
	So she was sent to *Light's Redemption*, a prestigious boarding school ran by an internationally renowned monastic order, high up in the mountains and functionally cut off from the world. The first year, she learned nothing. So her family paid for another year. On the second year, she learned how to embezzle cutlery from the students' canteen and sell it. On the third year, she made a business of selling off the things newly departed students had left behind when they moved out. Soon, "Sally" was the girl every student came to for aid with all their problems. Whether it was getting a sneak peek at the exam paper questions or smuggle a love letter to the right recipient, Sally knew how. Or found a way. 
	
	The Tarrants couldn't well take her back as the years went by. They could only hope she'd finally learn and come back reformed. Sally was on her tenth year, and basically an inventory item, trapped in a small cloistered environment she had made her own. But she was also very much "the odd one" at campus. Part student, part... something else. At 25, she had drifted far from the younger students. Until she met someone...
	
	Sally is made to be a Paladin with a twist. She's met someone at the academy who became so special to her that she's willing to lay down her life for them, basically turning her entire life around. Now they're her sacred Ward, and she will employ an absolutely spectacular toolkit of both brawn and trickery to keep them from safe from all harm.
	
	*Speak with your fellow players and synchronize your backstories, to decide whom in your party Sally has decided to devote her life to keeping safe, and why. This part is left open in order to allow you to decide between yourselves how it should play out, according to the characters and backstories available at your table.*
	
	## Personality
	Sally is a weird paladin, as far as pallies go. She's an opportunist and a trickster, bringing roguish charm to everything from negotiations to bar fights. Even though she has the tactical mind of a professional charlatan, she loves flirting, joking and carousing along with the martials. Play Sally if you want a devoted bodyguard with a clear purpose who still knows how to have a good time‚Äîand whose definition of 'protection' might occasionally need discussing.
	
	Leaving the monastery for the first time would be a harrowing and deeply scary experience for Sally. She was sent to monastery jail for ten years and carved out her own niche in the mountains, but now she needs to truly apply herself. The first time she needs to protect her ward, or the party her ward decided to travel with, may be when she first realizes exactly what she signed up for.
	
	Sally without a cause would be running cons in the student cafeteria until she's 50. Sally *with* a cause however, is a person cheerfully throwing herself in front of swords, fire breaths and eldritch blasts. That's not a flaw she needs to overcome. On the contrary, that's her whole engine, and it's dangerous, not because Sally would be willing to die for someone. Rather, It‚Äôs that she might decide, without asking, what they need saving from.
	
	Sally needs external purpose or she calcifies. Give her a mission and she's unstoppable. Take it away and she'll build another comfortable cage. She's externally motivated by nature, which makes her both intensely loyal and potentially terrifying, because what happens if her purpose disappears?
	
	If you want to increase the depth and the drama at your table, Sally could have some problematic older sibling traits that invite dynamic play in your group. She' could be the one who "handles" problems before they reach her ward. The one who decides what they need to know, and who smothers with care while calling it protection. 
	
	She makes tea and makes sure her ward is always warm and comfortable, but she also stalks the tavern wench or servant who made her ward uncomfortable or behaved in any way suspiciously. She's not sinister about it, she genuinely cares, but a bodyguard-trickster with an obsessive work ethic is one bad day away from doing something her ward never asked for and can't take back.
	
	The house Tarrant may indeed be happy that Sally finally wandered off. But should she ever cross paths with them again, that may awaken many painful questions about rejection. She is who she is: Big, rambunctious and generally kind-hearted, and they apparently could never forgive her for that.
	
	One of the deepest and most difficult questions, for more character driven roleplay: **what is Sally's deepest fear?** Scale. The world getting too big, too uncontrollable. Threats she can't anticipate. She came to the monastery, built herself a throne and then just sat there in a space small enough to control completely. Now the Oath is her new cage, but she's the one holding the key. The Ward may grow beyond her ability to protect them, or worse, one day they might not even need protection anymore. And if the Ward doesn't need her, then Sally's just... what? A 25-year-old failed student ex-embezzler with a hammer and nowhere to be.
	
	Sally probably knows, on some level, that she's doing it again. Building another monastery operation. Another small but legible place where she gets to be essential by making herself the only solution. The Oath gave her divine power, sure, but it also gave her permission to never have to figure out who she is without a job to do.
	
	**Personality traits**
	Solving problems is half the fun. Making trouble is the other half.
	
	**Ideals**
	Loyalty: The one person who saw me doesn‚Äôt get to regret it. Ever.
	
	**Bonds**

	## Playing Sally
	Reflavor the Oath of Redemption from "Divine Peacekeeper" to "Personally invested bouncer". Why the Gods accepted her oath she does not know, and rightly does not care much about. As long as they provide her with the power to shut down any threat to her Ward, she's all for it. This is her life now, and she has so many years of purposeless existence to make up for. The Redemption Paladin can take damage for others, reduce threats, buff allies and work as secondary healer if need be. She may not be a perfect fit for the subclass looking at the exact tenets of the Oath of Redemption, but keep the sub-class in its entirety and rework the tenet: Protect Ward at all costs. 
	
	The Ward needs to be someone who can give Sally something to actually, truly believe in. Whatever grants her these powers, the Oath needs true conviction. She needs to feel like this is her chance to become something more, or to give her life to something that feels right in every bone of her body. And that suggests that an idealist has lived in there all the time. But it didn't have any oxygen before. It was smothered in monk jail.
	
	**Suggestions for what the Ward relationship could be:**
	
	*The Idealist* ‚Äì Ward believes in something Sally never dared to. She borrows their certainty.
	*The Fragile One* ‚Äì Ward is competent but wounded or in some capacity disabled. Sally overcompensates.
	*The Mirror* ‚Äì Ward is independent, maybe even reckless. Sally protects against their freedom.
	
	*Fighting style: If your Ward has low AC, it's better to switch out Protection for Interception.*

## Session zero notes

Sally is built to be a charismatic, roguish Paladin who for the first time in her life believes in something. If you're going to play her as full "toxic helicopter mom" you **need to okay this with the table, and especially with your assigned Ward**. You and your fellow player will be essential to each other with these characters, and you must absolutely make sure that you're both on board with this scenario and dynamic. A good idea is to invite the table to help you know where the line is. This gives your fellow players express permission, allowing them to feel safe to let you know if Sally accidentally crosses it.
