    Mahorran the Undertide¶

    "I'm here because the King of the Sea told me to. I'm his agent. And as long as he wants me with you, I'll keep blasting and saving your asses. But this is a strictly professional relationship."

    A corporate Marid's one-woman pirate crew grinding it out on the high seas for that sweet promotion to branch manager. She boards ships in Darkness, wins fights by equal amounts of intimidation and drowning tactics, and retreats to her underwater garden for depressingly uneventful downtime.

    Character Overview¶

    Species: Sea Elf
    Class: Warlock 5 (The Genie â€“ Marid)
    Background: Charlatan
    Age: 33
    Alignment: Neutral Evil

    Quick Intro¶

    Quick Intro

    At the Table

    Initially, Mahorran is aggressively professional towards the party. She only really makes conversation when she interprets they're having a mandated break from work and she's expected to be social, and excuses herself to her vessel the moment her workday is up and social interaction becomes optional. The mystery isn't whether she has an evil patron, that's the premise. The big question is whether there's anything underneath the job description at all. Play Mahorran if you want to take a more laidback position in the group and still get to make an impact, with a relatively easy to play character.

    Backstory (Short Form)

    Mahorran operates as a pirate (job title: enforcer-collector) for a Marid who styles itself King of the Sea. She boards merchant ships with ruthless efficiency, and subdues or convinces crews to surrender magical cargo. Now her patron has ordered her to embed with an adventuring party for reasons she doesn't understand, and she's stuck making small talk around campfires when she'd rather be repotting kelp in her Genie's vessel. She can't help but suspect she must have done something to make her patron cross.

    Playing Mahorran

    • Combat: Excellent battlefield controller, standard Warlock damage output, downright lethal in sea campaigns. Darkness/Devil's Sight are a classic combo, but don't always make her the best team player; she is equipped to work well on her own.
    • Roleplay: Terse, professional, mildly annoyed by social obligations. Sees herself as a normal wage slave, hoping for promotion within a year or two. She's a woman of simple pleasures, genuinely lacking in imagination and creativity. Her big recreative outlets are gardening kelp in her saltwater terrarium Genie Vessel, and reading truly cheap literature, from bodice rippers to werewolf pulp. She much enjoys the werewolf pulp. Is entitled to one free day a week and will complain loudly if circumstances demand she adventure on her downtime.
    • Party Synergy: Reliable in combat, follows orders and keeps people alive as per the job description, but bonding requires the party to actively drag her into engagement.

    Deep Dive¶

    Deep Dive

    The Unseen Pirate¶

    Mahorran's method of piracy is simple and efficient. She scouts ships holding artifacts her patron may want using Detect Magic, stalks them by swimming after or hitching a ride underneath. Then, at night, at the waterline, she attacks. An attack with Thorn whip and a Telekinetic Pull pulls a defender 15 ft, and gravity hands them to the ocean. Magical Darkness rolls in over board, she climbs up acrobatically and starts blasting from the Darkness, protected by Mage Armor. Repelling Eldritch Blasts send more defenders overboard.

    Then, a voice booms from the dark, calm and amplified by Thaumaturgy, issuing instructions: Drop the weapons, fall to your knees, and stay just so... the King of the Sea demands it. If they comply, the fight ends early and Mahorran goes in her dark shroud to collect any Magic items in the cargo. Shoving her Genie's Vessel, a coral ring, through a hatch or window below deck, she disappears into the ring, which plummets discreetly into the water and is collected by her octopus familiar. By the time the Darkness lifts, the deck is quieter, the crew smaller, and no one is quite sure what they were fighting, only that it came from below, and it will likely do so again.

    When the crew fights back, Mahorran resorts to Hypnotic pattern for crowd control, picking the crew off one by one. If they manage to overwhelm her, Thunderstep serves as her final statement before she plummets into the sea and disappears.

    Personality¶

    Mahorran is neutral evil in the specific sense that she's professional about violence. She doesn't enjoy killing. It's inefficient, messy, and usually unnecessary when terror does the same job faster. Her preference is always the path of least resistance: scare or enchant them into compliance, collect the cargo, leave. The cruelty is structural, bureaucratic, not personal.

    Outside of work, she's an introvert who has spent years operating in darkness and silence, interfacing with people only long enough to scare them into compliance. Sustained friendly interaction is a skill she never developed. When the rogue asks her to keep watch together, she doesn't understand that's a bonding opportunity, she just rotates shifts efficiently and reports nothing of note. When the cleric tries to have a heart-to-heart about faith and patrons, she responds with, "It's a job. Faith has nothing to do with it. I'm not looking for grace, I'm looking for a promotion. Branch managers get a larger vessel, they say."

    She's not being cruel. She genuinely doesn't see the emotional dimension. And now she's supposed to learn it on the job while also keeping everyone alive, for reasons she doesn't know.

    Personality Traits: Flat honesty delivered like a contract addendum. The party has to shout at the octopus to get her attention when she's hiding away in the vessel.

    Ideals: Efficiency, hard work, the satisfaction of promotion and a good review.

    Bonds: Her patron (professional loyalty), her vessel garden (the only thing she maintains for herself), andâ€”eventually, reluctantlyâ€”the party members who keep treating her like a person despite her best efforts.

    Flaws: Under-communicates.

    The Grind¶

    Mahorranâ€™s relationship to her patron is structured explicitly as employment. Her Pact of the Tome serves double duty as a ledger for entering hauls, tracking profit over time and mapping expenses or dips in performance. She has a fixed work schedule, including one designated day off per week during which she will not undertake adventuring activities unless circumstances force her to. If she does work on this day, she will track overtime. She can't work properly unless they adhere to agreed terms.

    Her patron evaluates her performance through regular reviews. These assessments prioritize efficiency, compliance, recovery of targeted assets, and avoidance of unnecessary complications. Mahorran is acutely aware of these metrics even in combat situations, occasionally adjusting tactics based on what she believes will "read well" in a review. She competes with at least one rival warlock for standing and promotion, and underperformance carries the implicit threat of reassignment or termination.

    At one point, her patron requests satisfaction feedback from the adventuring party as part of an upcoming evaluation. Mahorran presents this request factually and without emotional framing, but the moment represents genuine vulnerability: external assessment by peers is rare in her work, and negative feedback has tangible consequences for her professional future. She does not ask whether the party likes her; she asks whether she met expectations. Criticism is internalized as data, not as a personal attack, even when it clearly affects her.

    The Vessel¶

    The Genie's Vessel is a ring worn around one of her octopus familiar's tentacles. She spends more time inside than strictly necessary, because she thinks a lot, plans a lot, and doesn't really like people. The vessel is an opulent saltwater terrarium, three feet of seawater cover the floor, kept oxygenated by living plants she's cultivated lovingly. Kelp anchored to the walls sways gently even though there's no current. Bioluminescent algae clings to surfaces, casting warm green light. Small anemones attach to stones. Slow-moving starfish and conches drift along the bottom. She even keeps turtles. Found them as hatchlings, brought them in "temporarily," never released them.

    When someone enters for the first time, they step through expecting a room, furniture, floorâ€”and instead they're wading through waist-deep saltwater, kelp brushing against their legs, tiny fish darting away. Mahorran's sitting in her hammock strung just above the waterline, and she has to watch them process the fact that her "home" is a beautiful aquarium she lives inside. The contrast with her professional life is devastating.

    "I'm here because the King of the Sea told me to. I'm his agent. This is the assignment."

    "Only one more Magic item from a new monthly record. I must say, this hasn't been my worst assignment yet."

    "You want to take me... bar... hopping? Is this extracurricular activities? What's the policy and expectations exactly? And uh, do they serve cider?"

    "I'd like to meet a werewolf one day. No reason really. Just curious what they smell like when they get wet."

    "Of course my octopus doesn't have a name. It's a tool. But if you're gonna go ahead and call it anything, I guess Pierre will do. But don't."

    "You're less annoying than most dry-folk." (This is the closest she gets to expressing affection.)

    [When asked about the turtles] "They came with the vessel." (They absolutely did not.)

    Themes and Inspirations¶

    The "late capitalism warlock": her patron is a boss extracting her soul and personhood through artificial competition and the constant threat of being replaceable. She's not enslaved by pact magic, more like she's just scared of losing her job. She's tragic in the way a middle manager is tragic: capable of more, settling for less, too indoctrinated to imagine there could be other alternatives. Until suddenly she's surrounded by people who haven't given up on wanting things, and she has to decide if she's going to let that affect her.

    Key Relationships

    The Marid (Patron): The Marid styles itself King of the Sea and runs a tight "The Office" style business. Mahorran is openly proud of the arrangement in a way most warlocks aren't. No shame, no existential crisis, just employment. Which is a good thing. If you're employed, you're contributing, right? The patron wants magical items collected, ships inspected, and its authority performed across the waves. In return, she gets power and continued existence. It's transactional, professional, and she prefers it that way.

    Why the Marid ordered her to join this particular party remains unclear. Maybe someone's carrying an item it covets. Maybe the party is heading toward something the Marid has interest in, or are uniquely placed to avert something the Marid fears. Maybe it's a test. Mahorran doesn't know, doesn't ask, and the uncertainty gnaws at her more than she admits.

    Galvan The Riptide (Rival Warlock): The Riptide has been competing with Mahorran for years, and the King of the Sea plays them against each other deliberately for the role of branch manager. Both know that falling too far behind means being "cancelled"â€”terminated from the arrangement in whatever way genies terminate disappointments. Galvan The Riptide is far worse than Mahorran. Crueler, more willing to escalate, less interested in efficient solutions. As a result, he harvests more Magic items, but he also gets in a lot more trouble. Somehow, Mahorran has managed to keep up, but the pressure is real.

    Notes for the DM

    Dramatic Questions¶

    The core tension isn't whether Mahorran is good or evilâ€”it's whether she's anything beyond her job description? Does she have values independent of the patron's orders? Does she care about people, or only about the grind? The vessel garden suggests capacity for care exists. The party provides the first opportunity to apply it to beings that can talk back.

    Adventure Hooks¶

    The Riptide Surfaces. A ship the party needs to investigate was already hit by someone using similar tactics: Darkness, drowning, forged writs, but messier, more bodies. Mahorran recognizes the signature. Her rival is operating nearby, which means either competition or collaboration, and she doesn't know which the King of the Sea wants.

    Cargo of Interest. The party acquires a magical item the Marid explicitly wants. Mahorran's orders are clear: secure it and deliver. But the item matters to someone in the party, or has moral implications for its use. Professional obligation versus emerging loyalty.

    The Termination Notice. The King of the Sea sends word that Mahorran's performance metrics are slipping. The Riptide is outperforming her. Her position is under review. Does she escalate her methods to compete, or does she start considering whether employment with this particular boss is worth preserving?

    Running the Character¶

    Mahorran's evil alignment manifests as professional amorality rather than active cruelty. She's not going to betray the party for funâ€”that would be inefficient and compromise the mission. She's dangerous when orders conflict with party interests, not when she gets bored.

    The social awkwardness is comedy, not obstruction. She participates in party activities badly rather than refusing entirely. She tries; she's just terrible at it. Let players find that endearing rather than frustrating.

    The vessel garden is her vulnerability. Guarding it fiercely early; allowing someone in is major character development. Don't rush it.

    • Agonizing Blast: +4 damage per Eldritch Blast attack.

    • Devil's Sight: 120 ft. vision in magical and nonmagical darkness.

    Pact of the Tome¶

    The Book of Shadows contains three additional cantrips: Druidcraft (for telling weather at sea, working with her kelp garden and snuffing out torches before a raid), Thaumaturgy (for bonus to Indimidation checks to make ships surrender, makes life easier), Thorn Whip (good for quickly pulling a guard overboard to drown them) and two ritual spells (Find Familiar, Detect Magic). The octopus familiar is essential to her operating pattern as it can carry her vessel ring on its tentacle. Detect magic is used to scout ships for magic items before she decided if they're a mark.

    Genie Features¶

    Genie's Vessel (Ring): Bonus action to enter. Up to 6 hours inside. Interior is a 20-ft cylinder. Her vessel is a 3 ft deep saltwater terrarium she's cultivated into a beautiful living ecosystem.

    Bottled Respite: The primary escape route. She can vanish into the ring, the octopus does a deep dive, and she's untraceable.

    Genie's Wrath: +3 cold damage once per turn on attacks.

    Equipment¶

    Daggers (2), Net, Orb (spellcasting focus), Forgery Kit, Backpack, Books (navigation charts, collected writs, downtime relaxation reading)

    Suggested Magic Items:

    • Cloak of Elvenkind reinforces stealth-boarding tactics.
    • Rope of Entanglement adds another control option.
    • Ring of Water Walking is thematically appropriate but mechanically redundant given her swim speed.
    • Wand of Web or similar control items synergize with her battlefield-shaping approach.

    Spellcasting¶

    • Spell Save DC: 15 | Spell Attack: +7 | Pact Slots: 2 (3rd level)
    • Cantrips: Eldritch Blast, Blade Ward, Minor Illusion, Druidcraft, Thaumaturgy, Thorn Whip
    • 1st Level: Armor of Agathys, Hex; Mage Armor (at will via invocation); Find Familiar, Detect Magic (rituals via Tome)
    • 2nd Level: Suggestion, Darkness
    • 3rd Level: Hypnotic Pattern, Counterspell

    Spell Notes¶

    Suggestion lands best on stressed targets. "Drop your weapon and walk away" is reasonable when you've just watched two crewmates go overboard and can't see what's killing them.

    Hypnotic Pattern handles crowds when precise positioning won't work. Incapacitates clusters; she picks them off or bypasses them entirely.

    Counterspell protects the Darkness from being dispelled. If the enemy caster breaks her fog of war, she loses her advantage.

    Session Zero¶

    Session Zero Considerations

    Content Notes: Mahorran's neutral evil alignment means she'll follow party consensus for professional reasons but lacks intrinsic moral objections to methods other characters might find abhorrent. If the party needs a clear ethical floor, establish it in Session Zero rather than discovering conflicts mid-campaign.

    Patron Dynamics: The Marid-as-employer framing treats the warlock pact as a job rather than a soul bargain. This is played straight. She's not secretly resentful or looking for escape. If your table prefers Warlock patrons as sources of more existential horror than occupational dread, maybe adjust the Riptide rivalry and "termination" threat to carry more weight.
