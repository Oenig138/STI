    Graphic priorities

    Here’s a structured priority list for the Mud Nuns graphics pipeline, derived from the JSON and lore document you uploaded. It orders visual assets by narrative necessity, reuse value, and production efficiency.

    🎯 Tier 1 — Core Identity (Immediate Priority)¶

    Defines faction recognition, usable across all media (book, poster, GitHub repo, TTRPG handouts).

    1. Faction Icon / Logo

    2. Concept: Three overlapping bowls forming an infinity knot; steam rising.

    3. Use cases: Branding, corner sigil, watermark, print header.
    4. Formats: Vector (.svg) + hi-res raster (.png).

    5. Variants: Full color / black & white / turmeric monotone.

    6. The Humbler (Spectral Ladle)

    7. Purpose: Signature relic and light source; appears in lore and gameplay.

    8. Variants: Floating idle / serving / “correction” gesture.

    9. Lighting guide: Emits soft golden light; use as global illumination reference for other assets.

    10. Receipt Template

    11. Purpose: Printable prop and in-world motif.

    12. Design: Torn parchment texture, Mud Nun handwriting font, ladle stamp.
    13. Uses: Handout, quest item, sidebar graphic, promo asset.

    🧑‍🎨 Tier 2 — Character & Scene Definition (High Priority)¶

    Makes the order feel human and connects directly to story beats.

    1. Character Portraits

    2. Primary: Mother Esparda (elder Aasimar), Sister Demorah (Tiefling), 2–3 generic Sisters.

    3. Style: Half-body with expressive chiaroscuro lighting.

    4. Mood: Competence + compassion + street grit.

    5. Key Scene Illustration

    6. Scene: Soup line under bridge with The Humbler’s glow; stray animals; warm light amid ruin.

    7. Purpose: Visual thesis of the faction — combines doctrine, aesthetics, and atmosphere.

    8. Pot Network Icons

    9. Iron / Tin / Bronze / Copper Pots.

    10. Use: Maps, quick-reference graphics, UI overlays.
    11. Style: Minimal silhouettes, consistent with logo line weight.

    🏙️ Tier 3 — Environmental and Context Art (Mid Priority)¶

    Gives texture to campaign materials, perfect for spreads and posters.

    1. Environmental Spreads

    2. Concepts:

      • Bronze Pot Safehouse interior
      • Alley Soup Line at Dusk
      • Bridge Meeting (Esparda & Demorah)
      • Lighting: Use The Humbler as focal light; contrast Seekers’ cold steel-blue.

    3. Equipment Illustrations

    4. Items: Cookware set, holy symbol, turmeric robe texture, receipt stamp.

    5. Style: Clean spot illustrations for use in books or cards.

    ✍️ Tier 4 — Worldbuilding & Print Cohesion (Support Layer)¶

    Enhances immersion and visual coherence across media.

    1. Typography & Branding Pack

    2. Faction header font (IM Fell DW Pica / Cormorant Garamond Bold).

    3. Handwriting font overlay for receipts & margin notes.

    4. Apply parchment textures & drop caps from JSON spec.

    5. Texture & Layout Elements

    6. Subtle parchment/canvas overlays (5–10% opacity).

    7. Torn edge/margin note frames for printed pages.
    8. Small hand-drawn motifs (ladle, raccoon stripes, pot silhouettes).

    ❄️ Tier 5 — Deluxe & Atmospheric Expansion (Optional / Later)¶

    Useful for campaigns, posters, and lore compendiums after the visual language is locked.

    1. Fork Raid Poster or Zine Art

    2. Tone: Mischievous street propaganda (“If the rich can’t stoop to eat with their hands…”).

    3. Medium: Screenprint-style, limited palette.

    4. Contrast Faction Illustration

    5. Seekers of Penitence: Cool palette, vertical composition, clean chains.

    6. Purpose: Juxtapose with Mud Nuns for visual storytelling.

    7. Animal Companions Set

    8. Subjects: Dog, rat, raccoon, pigeon, stray cat — each carrying symbolic gestures or tools.

    9. Tone: Street-level loyalty, informal sainthood.
