    Elevator pitches

    Core criteria: dramatic clarity, narrative weight, immediacy of hook, and economy of language. This isn't about which one is "nicer" — it's about which one does the job better.

    Alveta "Velvet"¶

    Index: Air Genasi Glory Paladin. Her childhood stickfights became a scam dojo, now she fights to vindicate her dream.

    Character Page: A kid's fake fighting style got a boy killed. Now she's sworn an oath to make it real. Winning duels, teaching it right, proving that a dream born from a scam can still come true. Meanwhile, someone's franchising the dangerous version, and she's pretty sure it's the childhood friend she lost.

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    The index version reduces the stakes to mere "vindication." The character page immediately establishes someone died, creates moral tension ("fake fighting style that killed"), and introduces the ghost in the machine (the franchising rival who might be the lost friend). The index pitch is functional; the character pitch has teeth.

    Basil Tenebrian¶

    Index: Noble Bladesinger Shifter, hunted by his brother, but still a Good Boy. Struggles with body dysmorphia and clings to decency like a drowning man.

    Character Page: Shifter Bladesinger who spent his isolated youth studying swordplay in secret, certain he'd be ready when danger came. His mentor died proving he wasn't. Now he's hunted, his mother imprisoned, and the wallflower dandy who imagined himself 'a scholar riding the wolf' is learning that real life seldom respects fantasies.

    Verdict: Character page wins, but needs trimming.

    The index version is too cute ("Good Boy") and buries the actual dramatic architecture. The character page names the illusion-reality gap that defines Basil and gives concrete stakes (dead mentor, imprisoned mother). However, it's bloated. Cut "isolated youth studying swordplay in secret" down to essentials, keep the mentor death and the fantasy-reality collision. Rewrite to 2-3 sentences max.

    Beitian "Betty" Winter's Heart¶

    Index: A Soulknife gambler from a rare, all-deaf clan who clings desperately to her self-image as Dragonchess prodigy, and lives for gossip and mustard.

    Character Page: White Dragonborn Soulknife from a clan born without earbones, where sign language and psionic gifts are the only speech. A Dragonchess prodigy who got hustled and can't go home until she's won back what she lost and interest that'll impress. So she fills her days with fast quips and other people's drama, because examining her own life would mean admitting maybe even a prodigy has her limits.

    Verdict: Hybrid approach needed.

    The index version is punchier but loses the hustled prodigy can't go home hook, which is the engine of the character. The character page is too verbose. Recommended synthesis: "Dragonchess prodigy from an all-deaf clan who got hustled and can't go home until she wins back her losses with interest. Lives for gossip and mustard because examining her own limits would shatter the self-image keeping her afloat."

    Belvarax "Gobsmack" Allimander¶

    Index: Dragonborn Paladin and party daddy scorned by his order for his awkward reinterpretation of his Vows, to not deal with raiding Goblins.

    Character Page: A paladin who read the fine print and realized "protect the weak" might mean the goblins, not the mining companies paying his order's bills to displace them. Now he's an unwelcome reminder that the oath answers to sincerity, not the rules of the order. Meanwhile the goblins keep dying while he's busy asking questions.

    Verdict: Character page wins decisively.

    The index version calls it "awkward reinterpretation" which undercuts the moral clarity. The character page names the institutional corruption (mining companies paying the order) and the tragic irony (goblins dying while he deliberates). This is not awkwardness; this is structural complicity. The character pitch respects that.

    Calypso "So-So" Sonder¶

    Index: Honorable Human Monk "kabuki luchador" chasing one last hurrah without selling out to vulgarity. Hogs party protein, but always apologizes.

    Character Page: Aging kabuki luchador so devoted to performing nobility he'd rather die than going 'vulgar' for the crowd. Can't bring himself to tell the truth about why he was fired from the circus, chasing one last hurrah.

    Verdict: Hybrid needed.

    The index has the "hogs protein, apologizes" detail which is characterizing gold. The character page has the dramatic weight (would rather die, can't tell truth about firing). Synthesis recommended: "Aging kabuki luchador who'd rather die than go vulgar for the crowd. Hogs party protein but always apologizes. Can't tell the truth about why he was fired, chasing one last hurrah before his body gives out."

    Cog-Stool¶

    Index: Warforged Spore Druid, a strange two-minded chimera born of centuries of quiet interfacing between a decommissioned battledroid prototype and an awakened mycelial network.

    Character Page: A decommissioned warforged lightning rod colonized by mycelium for centuries. Somehow an interface arose, and now two incommensurable minds with vastly different perspectives and powers share one frame.

    Verdict: Index wins slightly, but both need work.

    The index version is clearer about the dual-mind premise. The character page's "somehow an interface arose" is vague hand-waving. But both suffer from being concept-focused rather than dramatic tension-focused. What's the problem of being two minds? What do they want that conflicts? This needs a third option that introduces friction between the minds or between Cog-Stool and the world.

    Clover Medley¶

    Index: Tiefling Ranger. Charming farmgirl from a loving home in a deeply cursed valley, obsessed with chasing an alleged blighted Kirin. Wrangles Abyssal pigs and creeps out the cityfolk.

    Character Page: Charming Tiefling farmgirl from a blighted valley where the farm pigs grunt in Abyssal. The locals think her birth another sign of the curse. Her family knows better. Her grandpa swore on his deathbed he saw a "cursed Kirin" poisoning the land, and it has become her White Whale.

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    The index buries the emotional core (locals blame her, White Whale obsession). The character page establishes the social tension (blamed for the curse despite family knowing truth) and the deathbed promise driving her quest. Keep the character page version; it has narrative architecture the index lacks.

    Donner¶

    Index: Harengon Battle Master haunted by the apprentice he sacrificed to escape a Revenant; keeps others alive with blunt advice and kale stew.

    Character Page: A camp cook who spent ten years watching heroes die for glory, then sacrificed his own apprentice to escape the thing that killed them. Now he keeps new companions alive with grim stories and good stew, and can't tell anymore if it's love or just making sure someone's around to throw behind him when the Revenant catches up.

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    The index is functional but flat. The character page gives you the full moral calculus: ten years of watching glory-seeking death, the utilitarian sacrifice, and the devastating ambiguity ("love or just human shields?"). This is Donner's architecture—keep it.

    Geldok Iron Tusk¶

    Index: Soft-gutted Orc ex-raider turned unlikely Cleric of the mysterious "Big Boss"; streetcorner problem solver and healer, who obeys no worldly authority.

    Character Page: Ex-raider turned unlikely cleric of the mysterious "Big Boss". Street-corner theologist who doesn't believe in preaching, instead opting to be a healer and problem solver for the little guy.

    Verdict: Index wins.

    "Soft-gutted" and "obeys no worldly authority" do more characterization work than "doesn't believe in preaching." The index has texture; the character page is generic. Keep index version.

    Harold Carnap¶

    Index: Halfling Warlock grandpa. Devilishly wholesome, turning blood rituals to potlucks. Possibly an evil genius in a cardigan. The jury is still out.

    Character Page: Halfling Warlock grandpa who accidentally became a cult leader by turning blood rituals into potluck dinners and community daycare. May be an evil genius.

    Verdict: Index wins narrowly.

    "Accidentally became a cult leader" is good, but the index's "the jury is still out" respects the ambiguity better. The character page settles the question too much ("may be"). Use index but steal "accidentally became a cult leader" if you rewrite.

    Iblit¶

    Index: Calvin & Hobbes meets Good Omens: An orphaned teen Kobold Celestial Warlock bound to an archangel who hates this assignment as much as he does.

    Character Page: Calvin & Hobbes meets Good Omens: A kobold teen warlock bound to an archangel who hates this assignment as much as he does. Radiant damage, theological bickering, and occasional outbursts of "you're not my mom!"

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    The index says "orphaned" which is redundant (the archangel dynamic implies abandonment issues). The character page adds concrete flavor ("you're not my mom!") that sells the relationship dynamic immediately. Keep character page.

    Inakha Nulakiano¶

    Index: Conservative Goliath working-class Ranger chasing culinary redemption armed with a greataxe, a mysterious cookbook, and the emotional regulation skills of an average toddler.

    Character Page: Goliath monster hunter chasing culinary redemption for her exiled family, armed with a greataxe, a legendary cookbook, and the emotional regulation of a kicked hornet's nest. Conservative workhorse who sees a Gelatinous cube as aspic with extra steps.

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    "For her exiled family" adds stakes. "Kicked hornet's nest" is better imagery than "toddler." "Gelatinous cube as aspic" is chef's— fuck. That line is inspired* and belongs in the pitch. Keep character page.

    Lia Amberhorn¶

    Index: Satyr Wild Magic Sorcerer who believes in her own stereotypes. Rejected by her sober, ableist merchant family, but still snipes with pinpoint precision, whether with word or spell.

    Character Page: Satyr Wild Magic Sorcerer who believes in her own stereotypes. She was rejected by her sober, ableist merchant family, but still snipes with pinpoint precision, whether with word or spell.

    Verdict: Identical. Index wins by being tighter (no "She was").

    These are the same pitch. Index version is slightly cleaner prose. Use that.

    Mahorran the Undertide¶

    Index: A corporate Marid's one-woman pirate crew grinding it out on the high seas for that sweet promotion to branch manager. Precious about her working conditions and depressingly uneventful downtime.

    Character Page: A corporate Marid's one-woman pirate crew grinding it out on the high seas for that sweet promotion to branch manager. She boards ships in Darkness, wins fights by equal amounts of intimidation and drowning tactics, and retreats to her underwater garden for depressingly uneventful downtime with her turtles.

    Verdict: Hybrid needed.

    "Precious about working conditions" is better character shorthand than the tactical rundown. But "turtles" is a concrete detail worth keeping. Synthesis: "Corporate Marid's one-woman pirate crew grinding for that sweet branch manager promotion. Precious about her working conditions and retreats to her underwater garden for depressingly uneventful downtime with her turtles."

    Orfan¶

    Index: Firbolg Archaeologist whose questionable scientific methods landed him Draconic Sorcery powers. Torn between the call of the wild and the academy that failed him.

    Character Page: The academy dismissed his archaeological methods, the coven exploited his trust. Returning to the forest and walking the ancient paths, his 'bad academic praxis' yielded him Draconic powers. Now he's stuck between his love of simple life and longing for recognition from the faculty that failed him.

    Verdict: Character page wins, but needs trimming.

    The index is too vague ("questionable methods"). The character page names the dual betrayal (academy dismissal, coven exploitation) that defines Orfan's wound. But it's wordy. Rewrite suggestion: "Firbolg archaeologist dismissed by the academy and exploited by the coven. His 'bad praxis' yielded Draconic powers, but he's torn between wilderness peace and the academic recognition that still haunts him."

    Salynna "Sally" Tarrant¶

    Index: Trickster Paladin who stole cutlery at the boarding school for ten years before she found her purpose in life; now smothers her sworn Ward with an overreach of steel and cunning.

    Character Page: Gifted Human Trickster Paladin sent by her noble family to a boarding school, hoping to file down the "rough edges". Instead she started her own semi-criminal operation for ten full years before purpose found her; now she is the ultimate bodyguard, smothering her Ward with an overreach of competence, cold steel and street hustling.

    Verdict: Index wins.

    The character page front-loads setup ("sent by noble family to boarding school") that doesn't pull weight. The index gets to the point: ten years of theft, then found purpose, now overcompensates. Cutlery theft is specific and memorable. Keep index.

    Scarecrow¶

    Index: Warforged Bard who stood guard in a village square for centuries, quietly indexing every life and family tree. Gives great hugs. Also spies on you.

    Character Page: A warforged infiltration-construct who stood disguised in a village square for centuries, indexing every life. Still hard-wired to spy on everybody it loves, it now offers great hugs and subdues threats with overwhelming psionic force.

    Verdict: Index wins.

    "Infiltration-construct" is clunky. "Subdues threats with overwhelming psionic force" is mechanical detail that doesn't belong in an elevator pitch. The index's "Gives great hugs. Also spies on you" is economical, unsettling, and memorable. Keep it.

    Sergeant Bink¶

    Index: Bugbear Rune Knight sheriff who sees patterns in everything, talks to his corkboard, and eats his own paperwork. Your local cryptid with badge authority. The town children love him anyway.

    Character Page: Your local cryptid with badge authority. A bugbear sheriff who sees patterns everywhere, talks to his corkboard, and struggles with paperwork. Your classic maverick cop trope poured into the chassis of a fey outsider who only tries to be a valuable member of the community.

    Verdict: Index wins.

    The character page's "classic maverick cop trope poured into chassis" is meta-commentary, not pitch. It breaks the fourth wall unnecessarily. The index's "eats his own paperwork" and "children love him anyway" are concrete and endearing. Keep index.

    Sister Garra¶

    Index: Tiny Tabaxi Trickster Cleric of the illegal slum order the Mud Nuns. Smuggles refugees and leaves receipts of spiritual unclogging with those she robs.

    Character Page: Illegal slum cleric of the Mud Nuns and refugee smuggler who redistributes wealth with bureaucratic zeal, racing against middle age while her order pressures her to retire from hustling and take the scholarly "turmeric robe".

    Verdict: Character page wins (barely).

    "Racing against middle age" and the "turmeric robe" retirement pressure add dramatic urgency the index lacks. But "bureaucratic zeal" is weaker than "leaves receipts of spiritual unclogging." Synthesis: "Tiny Tabaxi of the illegal slum order the Mud Nuns. Smuggles refugees, redistributes wealth, leaves receipts of 'karma rebalanced.' Racing middle age while the order pressures her to retire to the scholarly turmeric robe."

    Stennart Goldgift¶

    Index: Mediocre Dwarf merchant accidentally turned overnight Hercules. Slowly transforming into a World Tree seedling, but still clings to the strength that finally made him feel significant.

    Character Page: Mediocre Dwarf merchant accidentally turned heroic overnight. Slowly transforming into a World Tree seedling while desperately clinging to the strength that finally made him feel special. He knows he's cheating, he's made his peace. Now he intends to live out an unforgettable finale.

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    "He knows he's cheating, he's made his peace. Now he intends to live out an unforgettable finale" is the thesis of Stennart's arc. The index says "significant"; the character page says "special" and "cheating" and "finale." That's the full architecture. Keep character page.

    Tormin Remus¶

    Index: "Guy Faust", a revolutionary who sold his soul and face to become the perfect insurrectionist, the pied piper of the smallfolk.

    Character Page: A revolutionary changeling warlock who traded his soul—and his face—to fight tyranny, now spreading sedition through traitorous tunes while slowly forgetting who he used to be.

    Verdict: Character page wins.

    The index's "Guy Faust" and "pied piper" are clever but don't do enough work. The character page names the tragedy: he's forgetting who he was. That's Tormin's architecture—the revolutionary who wins at the cost of selfhood. Keep character page.

    Yawp Cumuluni¶

    Index: Goliath Bard raised in silence, now a one-woman moshpit who plays bagpipes in thunderstorms and lives to be the hype machine that forces an echo from the world.

    Character Page: Goliath Bard raised in twelve years of enforced silence, now a force of nature and noise who plays bagpipes in thunderstorms and dares the world to respond.

    Verdict: Index wins.

    "One-woman moshpit" and "hype machine that forces an echo from the world" are vivid, visceral imagery. "Dares the world to respond" is weaker. "Twelve years of enforced silence" is good but doesn't outweigh the index's energy. Keep index.

    Summary Scoreboard¶

    • Character Page Wins: Alveta, Basil (trimmed), Belvarax, Clover, Donner, Iblit, Inakha, Orfan (trimmed), Stennart, Tormin
    • Index Wins: Geldok, Harold, Lia, Salynna, Scarecrow, Sergeant Bink, Yawp
    • Hybrid Needed: Betty, Calypso, Mahorran, Sister Garra
    • Both Fail (Need Rewrite): Cog-Stool

    The character pages generally win when they introduce stakes and moral architecture. The index wins when it favors economy and memorable imagery. Your instinct to create longer pitches for the character pages makes sense—they need to establish the dramatic problem. But many could be tightened without losing power.
